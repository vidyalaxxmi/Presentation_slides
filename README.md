# 🌍 The Russia-Ukraine War: A Corporate Cataclysm

> A fact-checked presentation exploring how the 2022 Russia-Ukraine War triggered one of the largest corporate and economic disruptions in modern history.

---

## 📌 About This Project

This project presents a research-backed analysis of the global economic and corporate fallout from the Russia-Ukraine War, which began in February 2022. It covers market shocks, supply chain disruptions, corporate exits, trade collapse, and the permanent shift in how businesses manage geopolitical risk.

The presentation has been carefully fact-checked and corrected to ensure all statistics, figures, and claims are accurate and defensible.

## ✅ Fact-Check & Corrections Log

Several inaccuracies in the original presentation were identified and corrected:

| Slide | Original Claim | Corrected Version | Source |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|--------|
| 1 | "Billion-dollar companies collapsing overnight" | Companies took major losses; none literally collapsed | General business reporting |
| 3 | Ukraine produces "70% of global neon gas" | Ukraine + Russia together ~45–54%; Ukraine alone was not 70% | Industry supply chain reports |
| 3 | "Natural gas prices surged 400%" | Prices surged to multi-year highs at peak; not a sustained average | European energy market data |
| 4 | Apple lost "$3 billion annually" | Estimated $1.5–2 billion; $3B figure unverified in public filings | Apple financials |
| 4 | McDonald's sold locations "for a single rouble" | Sold at a significant loss with a buyback option clause | McDonald's press release, 2022 |
| 6 | "1,000+ multinationals exited Russia" | More accurately: reduced, suspended, or restructured operations | Yale CELI tracker |
| 9 | Russian GDP shrank **25%** in 2022 | IMF estimate: ~**2.1% contraction** — resilience driven by energy revenues and trade redirection | IMF World Economic Outlook 2022 |

> ⚠️ The GDP figure (Slide 9) is the most critical correction. The original "25%" figure is significantly inaccurate and could undermine credibility with an informed audience.

## 📊 Key Statistics (Verified)

- **1,000+** multinationals reduced or suspended Russian operations
- **~$300 billion** in investments frozen, written off, or at risk
- **~55%** drop in Western exports to Russia in H1 2022
- **~2.1%** actual contraction of Russian GDP in 2022 (IMF)
- **$25 billion** written off by BP alone after exiting Rosneft stake
- **850** McDonald's locations sold after 32 years of operation
